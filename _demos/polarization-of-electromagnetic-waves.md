### Description

A radar transmitting dipole is placed at the focus of a parabolic reflector and produces a plane wave directed at a detector several feet away. A wire grid is made of parallel wires running in only one direction. If the grid is placed between the transmitter and receiver with the wires parallel to the dipole antannae, there will be no signal to the receiver because the wires "short out” the electric field vector of the wave. If the wires of the grid are at right angles to the dipole axes and to the axis of the system, the signal is transmitted through the grid with very little attenuation.
