### Description

A tuning fork is mounted above a glass tube, the bottom end of which is closed by the level of water in a tall flask. The tuning fork and top tube are raised and lowered together, giving strong resonances when the length of the air column is an odd multiple of a quarter wavelength of the fork. If the frequency of the fork is known, the velocity of sound may be calculated from this known frequency and the measured wavelength.
