### Description

A large inductor has an iron core that extends from the top. An aluminum ring slides over the extended core. When the inductor is connected to an AC power source, the ring jumps into the air. The ring may then be cooled in liquid nitrogen and the experiment repeated. The ring will now jump much higher. The cooling lowers the resistance of the ring and allows more current to flow as well as altering the phase shift more favorably for continued repulsive forces. The result must be discussed in terms of the relative inductance and the reactance of the ring.
