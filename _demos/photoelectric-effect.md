### Description

This experiment shows the magnitude of the photoelectric effect and allows one to measure the energies of photons. The mercury light source, Hg, has a slit over its exit so that one can form a line spectrum with the prism P. The lens, L, focuses the tines on the phototube, T. The diaphragm D allows one to vary the intensity. The voltage to the photomultiplier is furnished by a battery and measured with the Keithley 160 Digital Voltmeter, K. The current is measured with an HP4254 ammeter, A, although it is usually used as a null instrument. One then measures the voltage for zero current as different lines of the mercury spectrum illuminate the tube. For good results, one should first have zero volts on the phototube and then find what setting of the diaphragm will give about the same photocurrent for each line of the spectrum.
